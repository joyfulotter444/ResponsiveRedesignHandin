Website Redesign

Introduction

For this project, I wanted to practice all parts of designing a responsive website's layout and visuals, starting from lofi-prototyping all the way through coding it with HTML and CSS Bootstrap.

I choose the website pnwx.com, a website for the radiation protection retailer PNWX (Pacfic Northwest X-Ray Inc.), because it still has the style of a website from decades ago and its layout is baffling for a modern internet user. Below I outline step by step how I go through the process of redesigning the website.

Analyzing Usability and Accessibility Problems

Before starting my lofi-prototyping, I analyzed the original website for usability and accesibility problems I wanted to fix.
To show accesibility issues, I ran the website through the Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool (WAVE).

    Usability Problems
  • Hierarchy: A lack of headers makes the page difficult to navigate.
  • Navigation: There is no way to navigate the page except through product links
  • Learnability: The arrow pointing to the search bar on the original website looks like a button but it's not
  • Memorability: There is nothing to remember the website by, not even an about section for the company
  • Mental Model: The websites cheesy font doesn't fit with what users expect from a serious company concerned about safety
  • Responsiveness: the website's layout and text doesn't change for different screen sizes, making it difficult to read on phones and tables
    Accessibility Problems
  • Missing alt text: The skull and arrow images are missing alt text, negatively impacting screen readers
  • Low contrast: Where the black text falls on a dark blue background the contrast is low, which could make it difficult to read
  • Lack of Headers: There are no header elements in the code of the page, so any hierarchy on the website that is communicated visually won't show up for screen readers

While I disagree with the low-contrast warnings, as I think all of the text is easily readable when it is an apprioprate size, I agree with all the other issues identified.

Lofi-Prototypes

I designed three lofi-prototypes to address the problems noted above, one prototype each for a phone, tablet, and laptop sized screen. The prototypes are annotated with what problems they solve.

Hifi-Prototypes

I designed three hifi-prototypes to plan out my website, one prototype each for a phone, tablet, and laptop sized screen. The prototypes are annotated with the Bootstrap classes and styles I plan to use

Closing Thoughts

Redesigning a website made me thinkg hard about what visual interfaces on the internet are supposed to accomplish. When looking at a href="http://www.pnwx.com/">pnwx.com for the first time, my first thought was that it was ugly. But after studying what made it difficult to use and creating my own design step by step, I realized that the aesthetics are not what caused the most friction for the user.
It was simple things, like items not being grouped into like categories, headings missing, and affordances misleading the user. While I worked hard to make the redesigned website looked pretty, and I learned a great deal about CSS, Bootstrap and HTML along the way, but now at the end of the process I've realized that even as a beginner, with no artistic experience to speak of, by taking the time to plan out where items go and how to group them in ways that express meaning you can accomplish a lot.
